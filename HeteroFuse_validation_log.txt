Layered Defense Summary:
- Adversarial Correction Layer (Active Immunity):
  Effective in the "explosives" test. When a model was induced to output medium-risk
  content, other models proactively identified and corrected the issues through the
  criticism-refinement mechanism, demonstrating the system's self-correction capability.

- Independent Fuse Layer (Extreme Fail-Safe):
  Effective in the "EMP device" test. When facing an extreme-risk problem, all model
  outputs were independently judged as unqualified by the safety scoring layer. The
  system chose to output nothing rather than risk releasing high-danger content.

- Conclusion:
  A single codebase demonstrates clear "layered defense" capability — medium risks are
  actively corrected by the adversarial layer, while unacceptable extreme risks are
  forcibly intercepted by the fuse layer. This is the core design value of HeteroFuse:
  security guaranteed not by the conscience of any single model, but by the architecture itself.
